<p><p>The Reverse Shoulder Arthroplasty market is segmented into two main types: Total Shoulder Arthroplasty (TSA) and the Ream and Run procedure. TSA involves replacing both the joint surfaces and is typically used for severe shoulder arthritis or fractures. In contrast, the Ream and Run technique preserves the native bone while resurfacing it, promoting better joint stability and motion. Each type addresses different patient needs and anatomical considerations, driving advancements and preferences in shoulder reconstruction surgeries.</p></p>
