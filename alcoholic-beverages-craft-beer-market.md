<p><p>The craft beer market is primarily divided into two main types: ales and lagers. Ales are brewed using top-fermenting yeast at warmer temperatures, resulting in a wide variety of flavors and aromas, often characterized by fruity and complex profiles. Lagers, on the other hand, utilize bottom-fermenting yeast at cooler temperatures, producing a cleaner, crisper taste with a smooth finish. Both categories showcase the creativity of craft brewers, appealing to diverse consumer preferences and driving growth in the craft beer industry.</p></p>
