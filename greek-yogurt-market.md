<p><p>Greek yogurt is a strained form of yogurt that has a thicker texture and higher protein content compared to regular yogurt. It is produced by removing the whey, resulting in a creamier consistency and a tangy flavor. Packed with probiotics, it is often favored for its health benefits, including improved digestion and increased satiety.</p><p>The Greek yogurt market is experiencing significant growth, driven by rising health consciousness among consumers and the increasing demand for high-protein snacks. The market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 10.10% during the forecast period. This growth is further fueled by the diversification of product offerings, including flavored and organic options, catering to a broader demographic.</p><p>Moreover, the trend towards plant-based and dairy-free alternatives has led to innovations in Greek yogurt substitutes made from almond, coconut, and soy. Retail channels are expanding as e-commerce gains traction, allowing consumers greater access to various Greek yogurt products. Additionally, there is a growing interest in functional foods that promote wellness, making Greek yogurt a popular choice among health-oriented consumers. Overall, the Greek yogurt market is poised for sustained expansion, reflecting changing dietary preferences and an increased focus on nutrition.</p></p>
